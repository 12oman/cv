Personal Statement

I am an artist, educator, and developer with a long-standing involvement in the arts and culture sector in Aotearoa. I have a high record of teaching excellence, engaging a diversity of student experiences and dispositions, enabling them to participate in meaningful learning with outstanding results.

I am looking to amplify my contribution to the education sector through enriching the connections between creativity, technologies and te ao Māori. I am a pioneering researcher at the intersection of emerging technologies, including machine learning and blockchain, and the application of Māori practices to contemporary pedagogies of educational strategy. This uniquely places me to support learning outcomes for a new generation of digitally advanced creatives.

I am an activator with a passion for the big picture. I am an empathic leader and enjoy fostering and being part of collaborative, fun and high-performing teams. Subtle concepts come naturally to me, and I am generous and confident in using Māori ways of knowing to share these to improve student outcomes and build on current thinking and practices.

I have experience designing and delivering from level 5 through to level 9 supervisions with PhD supervisions planned for 2023.

In the last three years I have built vibrant and diverse collaborative communities of practice while fostering relationships with industry stakeholders. I understand the growing necessity for sustainable creative pathways. My students at Orion Health, RocketLab, Xero, PwC, Nexbe .
