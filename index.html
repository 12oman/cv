Kaupapa

I am an artist, AI researcher and educator at the forefront of Aotearoa's creative and technology sectors. With a decade of teaching excellence, I engage diverse learners in transformative education that celebrates the interconnectedness of te ao Māori, art and emerging technologies.

As an empathic leader, I foster collaborative, high-performing teams and cultivate vibrant communities of practice while building relationships with industry. With experience designing and delivering curricula from levels 5 to 9, I am committed to enriching education at the nexus of creativity, technology, and the interconnected realms of te ao Māori and contemporary art, empowering the next generation of digital innovators and creative leaders.

I deeply understand the necessity for sustainable creative pathways. My students enjoy meaningful work at companies like RocketLab, Xero, PwC, Nexbe and more.

Professional Experience

Whitecliffe College

Joined as Lecturer (2020) in the School of Information Technology, advancing to Programme Leader (2021-2023) before focusing on a Lecturer role in the School of Design Innovation (2023-present). Led teaching across two Auckland campuses while implementing blended delivery methods. Expanded teaching team from two to five members. Chaired Staircasing and Progression Project, significantly improving student retention through application of graph database analysis. Oversaw integration of contemporary tech curricula resulting in increased enrollment. Successfully championed te ao Māori integration throughout curriculum. Key contributor to achieving Category 1 status in External evaluation and review (EER). Pioneered adoption of TouchDesigner and p5.js for interactive programming, alongside introducing Figma and low-code platforms to support visual thinkers.

Enspiral Dev Academy

Participated as Developer in conceptual development and implementation of full-stack projects (2019). Operated in a high-pressure Agile environment with an emphasis on continuous delivery. Developed human skills such as providing feedback and managing stress. Worked with technologies such as React, JavaScript, Node.js, and more.

University of Auckland

Began teaching at Elam School of Fine Arts as a Graduate Teaching Assistant (2012) while completing MFA, advancing to Professional Teaching Fellow and Course Co-ordinator (2014-2018). Lectured across major papers including Sculpture, General Studio, Time-based, Design, Drawing and Design Thinking. Recognized for ability to identify and develop subtle potentials in student work. Designed and led innovative projects combining real-world problems with creative outcomes. Pioneered the adaptation of Google Ventures' Design Sprint methodology in fine arts education, significantly improving student engagement and creative thinking. Demonstrated excellence in engaging diverse student experiences and dispositions. Successfully balanced artistic practice with teaching commitments, providing an effective model for students transitioning into creative careers. Extended teaching practice beyond traditional studio instruction to encompass broader contemporary art strategies and professional skills development.

Artspace Aotearoa

Started as Installation Technician (2009), advancing to Curatorial Assitant (2009-2011). Contributed to the development of significant contemporary art projects including international artist commissions and major exhibitions. Developed specialist skills in exhibition development, moving from technical installation to curatorial research and public program delivery. Engaged in institutional writing from funding applications through to exhibition texts and annual reporting. Coordinated complex stakeholder relationships across artists, funders, and public audiences while managing production budgets and project timelines.
